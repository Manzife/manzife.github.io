Pricing & Experimentation Lab

This space is a personal exploration of pricing strategies, experimentation methods, and decision-making under uncertainty. Pricing is treated as an inference problem: every price represents a hypothesis about customer behavior.

Here I experiment with statistical and economic methods used to measure demand, evaluate interventions, and optimize business outcomes.

Topics Explored

  • A/B Testing and Experimental Design
  • Price Elasticity Estimation
  • Causal Inference for Product Decisions
  • Revenue vs Conversion Trade-offs
  • Marketplace Pricing Dynamics

Methods & Techniques

  • Randomized Controlled Experiments (RCTs)
  • Difference-in-Differences
  • Regression Discontinuity Design
  • Bayesian Experimentation
  • Simulation-Based Pricing Models

Work in Progress

Upcoming projects include pricing simulations, experimentation notebooks, and applied studies connecting economic intuition with real-world product decisions.
